Empowering Women Through Education

The Women's Careers Foundation (WCF) exists to support the education and vocational or professional training of girls and women who need financial assistance to complete their studies. Our founders believed that "What is Worth Having is Worth Sharing". The charity works to raise and distribute funds in the form of grants to aspiring and talented girls and women who otherwise would lack the financial means to reach their educational goals.

1900s

Victorian Philanthropy

The Guild originated as an example of late Victorian philanthropy. S.H. Leeder started the Girls Realm Magazine in 1898 for girls of the leisured classes, becoming a means of communication for girls in isolated parts of the country who wanted to do something useful with their lives.

The Bishop of Stepney, Dr Winnington-Ingram, suggested the magazine's readers help their poorer fellows. The charity was founded in 1900, raising funds through bazaars, entertainments and social events.

The Fairy Ring Initiative

The Fairy Ring was established as the junior branch of the original guild, focused on providing financial support for pre-further education needs.

This innovative program engaged younger supporters in the mission of educational philanthropy, creating a pipeline of future advocates for women's education.

National Expansion

The Guild flourished with centres throughout the UK and Australia. Members raised funds through social events, establishing a trust fund for educating "poor girls of gentle birth."

1970s

Arts & Performance Legacy

Katherine Forbes-Dunlop joined the Committee in March 1973 and later established the Forbes-Dunlop Scholarships, awarded annually to candidates undertaking performing arts courses.

Modern Transformation

Adopted the name "Women's Careers Foundation" to reflect our broadened mission beyond "daughters of gentle folk" to supporting all women in need.

1985: The first Dorothy Carver Memorial Fund awards were granted. These smaller grants were made for purposes connected with educational, professional or vocational training courses outside the annual cycle for main grants.
